Referencias y Recursos

Fuentes primarias, artículos clave y recursos para profundizar

Fuente Principal

Asimov Press & Evan DeTurk (2026). “A Visual Guide to DNA Sequencing: How to ‘read’ nucleic acids, from Sanger to nanopores.” Asimov Press, Febrero 26, 2026.

Artículos Científicos Fundacionales

Secuenciación de Sanger

  1. Sanger, F. & Coulson, A.R. (1975). A rapid method for determining sequences in DNA by primed synthesis with DNA polymerase. Journal of Molecular Biology, 94(3), 441–448.

  2. Sanger, F., Nicklen, S. & Coulson, A.R. (1977). DNA sequencing with chain-terminating inhibitors. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 74(12), 5463–5467.

  3. Wu, R. & Taylor, E. (1971). Nucleotide sequence analysis of DNA. II. Complete nucleotide sequence of the cohesive ends of bacteriophage λ DNA. Journal of Molecular Biology, 57(3), 491–511.

  4. Maxam, A.M. & Gilbert, W. (1977). A new method for sequencing DNA. PNAS, 74(2), 560–564.

Piroseucenciación 454

  1. Nyren, P. & Lundin, A. (1985). Enzymatic method for continuous monitoring of inorganic pyrophosphate synthesis. Analytical Biochemistry, 151(2), 504–509.

  2. Ronaghi, M., Uhlen, M. & Nyren, P. (1998). A sequencing method based on real-time pyrophosphate. Science, 281(5375), 363–365.

  3. Margulies, M. et al. (2005). Genome sequencing in microfabricated high-density picolitre reactors. Nature, 437, 376–380. (Paper fundacional de 454 Life Sciences)

Illumina / Secuenciación por Síntesis

  1. Bentley, D.R. et al. (2008). Accurate whole human genome sequencing using reversible terminator chemistry. Nature, 456, 53–59.

  2. Turcatti, G. et al. (2008). A new class of cleavable fluorescent nucleotides: synthesis and optimization as reversible terminators for DNA sequencing by synthesis. Nucleic Acids Research, 36(4), e25.

PacBio SMRT

  1. Levene, M.J. et al. (2003). Zero-mode waveguides for single-molecule analysis at high concentrations. Science, 299(5607), 682–686. (Paper de los ZMWs de Webb y Craighead)

  2. Eid, J. et al. (2009). Real-time DNA sequencing from single polymerase molecules. Science, 323(5910), 133–138. (Paper fundacional de PacBio SMRT)

Nanoporos

  1. Kasianowicz, J.J., Brandin, E., Branton, D. & Deamer, D.W. (1996). Characterization of individual polynucleotide molecules using a membrane channel. PNAS, 93(24), 13770–13773.

  2. Bayley, H. (2006). Sequencing single molecules of DNA. Current Opinion in Chemical Biology, 10(6), 628–637.

  3. Laver, T. et al. (2015). Assessing the performance of the Oxford Nanopore Technologies MinION. Biomolecular Detection and Quantification, 3, 1–8.

Proyecto Genoma Humano

  1. Lander, E.S. et al. (International Human Genome Sequencing Consortium) (2001). Initial sequencing and analysis of the human genome. Nature, 409, 860–921.

  2. Venter, J.C. et al. (2001). The sequence of the human genome. Science, 291(5507), 1304–1351.

  3. Nurk, S. et al. (2022). The complete sequence of a human genome. Science, 376(6588), 44–53. (T2T — Genoma humano verdaderamente completo, 2021)

Revisiones y Artículos de Perspectiva

  2. Shendure, J. et al. (2017). DNA sequencing at 40: past, present and future. Nature, 550, 345–353.

  3. Amarasinghe, S.L. et al. (2020). Opportunities and challenges in long-read sequencing data analysis. Genome Biology, 21, 30.
